---
title: 'Self-pay Fee Schedule'
---

<h4>At a Glance</h4><p>Adding or updating your Self-Pay Fee items will allow Insights to automatically generate Patient Responsibility (PR) charges for self-pay patients, based on your organization’s established rates for specific CPT codes.</p><p>A self-pay fee schedule will free up considerable time for your administrative staff, as they will no longer need to manually look up CPT codes and rates.</p><p><strong>What you can do on your own</strong> Add or edit items in your schedule line by line.</p><p><strong>What you can do with help from an account manager</strong> Upload a fee schedule with multiple items. We can review and upload it for you. This is to ensure nothing gets broken in transition.</p><p>You can select whether you want post-visit PR generated for self-pay patients, based on your fee schedule. You can also choose the earliest date of service to which the fee schedule would apply, as well as a maximum self-pay charge per encounter.</p><img alt="" src="/images/self-pay-fee-schedule/self-pay-fee-schedule-1.png" /><h4><br /></h4><h4>How to Do It</h4><p>From the Patient Responsibility page, click the ‘Actions’ menu and choose <a href="https://insights.athelas.com/v2/patient-responsibility-settings">PR Settings</a>.</p><img alt="" src="/images/self-pay-fee-schedule/self-pay-fee-schedule-2.png" /><p>Click on the Self-Pay Fee Schedule tab. You may have to scroll the list of tabs to the right in order to see this tab.</p><p>From here, you can either use the Search function to find a CPT if you already have items in your fee schedule. For this walkthrough, start by clicking <code>Add Single Fee</code>.</p><img alt="" src="/images/self-pay-fee-schedule/self-pay-fee-schedule-3.png" /><p>Fill in the CPT Code, any modifiers, and the amount to charge in USD cents.</p><p>CPT modifiers (also referred to as Level I modifiers) are&nbsp;<strong>used to supplement the information or adjust care descriptions to provide extra details concerning a procedure or service provided by a physician</strong>. Code modifiers help further describe a procedure code without changing its definition. They are typically formatted as two characters, separated from other modifiers by a comma (For example: AB, CD)</p><img alt="" src="/images/self-pay-fee-schedule/self-pay-fee-schedule-4.png" /><p>Click Confirm.</p><p>Now you can view your new item in the fee schedule.</p><img alt="" src="/images/self-pay-fee-schedule/self-pay-fee-schedule-5.png" /><p>Click the pencil icon to modify the charge amount of the item, and the trash can to delete the item entirely.</p><p>❗ <strong>You will only be able to edit the charge amount of each item</strong>. If you would like to edit either the CPT code or modifiers, simply delete the item and add a new one.</p><p><br /></p><h4>Duplicate CPTs Codes and Modifiers.</h4><p>If you input an exact duplicate CPT code and modifier combination but with a different charge amount, the original will simply be updated.</p><p>If, however, you have a single CPT code, but different modifiers requiring different prices, the system will create a new line item.</p><img alt="" src="/images/self-pay-fee-schedule/self-pay-fee-schedule-6.png" /><p><br /></p><h4>Download Your Practice’s Self-Pay Fee Schedule</h4><p>In PR Settings, under the Self-Pay Fee Schedule tab, you can click <code>Download Fee Schedule</code> below the listed items in the fee schedule to get a CSV file. The data in this file can be viewed and manipulated in Excel or similar program.</p><img alt="" src="/images/self-pay-fee-schedule/self-pay-fee-schedule-7.png" /><p><br /></p><h4>Further Assistance</h4><p>We’re here to help!
