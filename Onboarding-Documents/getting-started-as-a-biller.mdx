# Working with Claims

The Claims Page is where you manage your entire claim workflow‚Äîfrom initial review to final submission. This page helps you process more claims per day with fewer errors by providing powerful filtering, AI-powered validation, and team collaboration tools all in one place.

**What you'll accomplish here:**

- Find and prioritize claims that need your attention
- Review and correct claim information before submission
- Catch errors automatically before they become rejections
- Assign work across your team to balance workload
- Track claim status from submission through payment

**Key benefits of using Athelas RCM:**

- **Fewer rejections:** AI validation catches errors before submission, reducing rework
- **Faster processing:** Custom views and bulk actions help you work through claims efficiently
- **Better visibility:** See exactly what changed, why, and who worked on each claim
- **Zero claims left behind:** Assignment tracking ensures every claim has an owner

## Setting Up Your Workspace

Before you start processing claims, set up custom views that match how you work. Think of views as personalized workspaces‚Äîeach one shows you exactly the claims you need to focus on for a specific task.

### Why custom views matter

Views save your filter combinations and display preferences so you can access them instantly. Instead of manually filtering hundreds of claims each time, you can switch to a saved view with one click.

### Creating your first filtered view

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start with a clear goal">
    Decide what type of work you're setting up this view for. For example: "I want to see all unassigned claims from Blue Cross Blue Shield worth over $500"

</Step>
  <Step title="Apply filters">
    Click the **Filter** button at the top of the Claims Page. You'll see dozens of filter options including:

- **Encounter Status:** Draft, Ready for Review, Approved, Submitted, Paid, Denied
- **Insurance Provider:** Filter by specific payers
- **Claim Amount:** Set minimum/maximum dollar amounts
- **Age:** How long the claim has been in the system
- **Assignment:** Assigned to you, assigned to others, or unassigned
- **Date ranges:** Creation date, submission date, payment date

Select the filters that match your goal. You can combine multiple filters‚Äîfor example, filter by both insurance provider AND claim amount AND assignment status.

<Tip>
Start with 2-3 key filters. You can always add more later as you refine your workflow.

## Daily Claim Management Workflow

This section walks you through the complete workflow for processing claims‚Äîfrom finding work to final submission.

### Finding claims to work

Your goal is to identify which claims need your attention right now. The Claims Page starts with all claims visible, which can be overwhelming. Use your saved views and filters to focus on what matters.

### Strategy 1: Start with your assigned work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your 'My Active Work' view">
    Select your saved "My Active Work" view from the Views dropdown. This shows all claims assigned to you that are ready for review or approved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prioritize and begin working">
    Sort by **Claim Amount** (highest first) or **Age** (oldest first), then click the first claim to open encounter details.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Strategy 2: Pick up unassigned work

If you've completed your assigned claims, open your "Unassigned Priority" view to find new work. Look for claims matching your expertise, then assign them to yourself (see next section).

<Tip>
Use the **Search bar** to find specific claims by patient name, claim ID, or provider name.
</Tip>

### Understanding claim statuses

As you review your filtered claims, you'll see various statuses. Here's what each means and what action you should take:

| Status | Meaning | Your Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Draft** | Claim information is incomplete | Complete missing fields before review |
| **Ready for Review** | Claim is complete and waiting for biller review | Review for accuracy, check for AI-flagged errors |
| **Approved** | Claim has been reviewed and is ready to submit | Verify one final time, then submit |
| **Submitted** | Claim has been sent to the payer | Monitor for acceptance or rejection |
| **Paid** | Payment received from payer | Reconcile payment, close claim |
| **Denied** | Payer rejected the claim | Review denial reason, correct and resubmit |

### Assigning claims

Assignment ensures every claim has an owner and prevents duplicate work. You should assign claims whenever you're about to work on them or when distributing work across your team.

### When to assign claims

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Before you start working" icon="user-check">
    Assign a claim to yourself before you begin reviewing it. This signals to your team that you're handling it and prevents someone else from working on the same claim simultaneously.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="When distributing workload" icon="users">
    If you're a team lead or helping balance work, assign claims to specific team members based on their expertise, workload, or the types of claims they typically handle.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Never leave claims unassigned for long" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Your goal is to keep fewer than 1% of open claims unassigned. Unassigned claims can fall through the cracks, age out, or result in duplicate work.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Assigning individual claims

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the claim">
    From your Claims Page list, find the claim you want to assign. You don't need to open the encounter details‚Äîyou can assign directly from the list view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the assignment dropdown">
    In the **Assigned To** column, click the dropdown for that claim. You'll see a list of all team members who can work claims.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the assignee">
    Click the team member's name. To assign to yourself, select your own name.

The claim is now assigned. You'll see the assignee's name appear in the **Assigned To** column immediately.

<Check>
**Verify:** The claim should now appear in that person's "My Active Work" view (or your own if you assigned it to yourself).
</Check>

</Step>
</Steps>
<Tip>
If a claim is already assigned to someone else but you need to take it over, simply assign it to yourself. The system will update the assignment and log the change in the claim's Change History.
</Tip>

### Bulk assigning multiple claims

When you need to assign many claims at once‚Äîfor example, distributing 50 new claims across a team of 5 billers‚Äîuse bulk assignment.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Filter to the claims you want to assign">
    Use filters to narrow down to exactly the claims you want to assign. For example, filter to show only unassigned claims from a specific payer.

<Warning>
Be careful with your filters. Whatever claims are currently visible will be affected by your bulk action.
</Warning>

</Step>
  <Step title="Select claims for bulk assignment">
    **Option A:** Check the box next to each claim you want to assign.

**Option B:** Click the checkbox in the table header to select all visible claims at once.

You'll see a count of selected claims appear at the top of the page (e.g., "15 claims selected").

</Step>
  <Step title="Click 'Bulk Actions' button">
    With claims selected, click the **Bulk Actions** button that appears at the top of the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose 'Assign'">
    From the bulk actions menu, select **Assign**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the assignee">
    Choose the team member who should receive all selected claims. All selected claims will be assigned to this single person.

<Info>
If you need to distribute claims across multiple people, repeat this process for each person with different filtered subsets of claims.
</Info>

</Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the assignment">
    Review the confirmation message showing how many claims will be assigned and to whom. Click **Confirm**.

<Check>
**Verify:** The **Assigned To** column should now show the assignee's name for all the claims you selected. The selected count should reset to zero.
</Check>

</Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Always double-check your filters before confirming a bulk action. Bulk assignment cannot be undone with a single action.
</Warning>

### Reviewing individual claims

Once you've identified a claim to work on and assigned it to yourself, open the encounter details to begin your review. Your goal is to verify all information is correct, fix any errors, and ensure the claim is ready for submission.

### Opening encounter details

Click any claim in your list to open the encounter details page. You'll spend most of your time on the **Content** tab, which shows patient demographics, diagnosis codes, procedure codes, and charges.

### Understanding AI validation and error flags

As you review a claim, the system automatically validates it and flags potential issues. This is one of your most powerful tools for catching errors before submission.

<Card title="What the sparkles mean" icon="sparkles">
Sparkles appear next to fields where AI has improved the claim to maximize reimbursement or ensure compliance.

**Common AI improvements:**

- Corrected zip codes based on patient address
- Added appropriate modifiers to procedure codes
- Updated place of service codes for accuracy
- Suggested diagnosis codes that better support the procedures

**What you should do:** Click the sparkle to see what changed and why. Review the AI's logic to ensure it makes sense for this specific case. You can accept or reject the change.
</Card>

<Card title="What error flags mean" icon="triangle-exclamation">
Red or yellow flags appear next to fields that have problems that must be fixed before submission.

**Common errors flagged:**

- Missing required fields (patient DOB, insurance member ID)
- Invalid diagnosis codes or procedure codes
- Conflicting information (e.g., date of service is in the future)
- Missing or invalid provider NPI numbers

**What you should do:** Fix the flagged field immediately. The claim cannot be submitted until all error flags are resolved.
</Card>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start with error flags">
    Scan the Content tab for any red or yellow warning icons. These indicate problems that will prevent submission or cause rejections.

Click each flag to see the specific error message and what needs to be corrected.

</Step>
  <Step title="Fix flagged errors">
    Update the incorrect or missing information directly in the encounter details. Common fixes:

- **Missing patient information:** Add DOB, address, or insurance details from your practice management system
- **Invalid codes:** Correct ICD-10 diagnosis codes or CPT procedure codes
- **Provider issues:** Verify and update provider NPI numbers or credentials

As you fix each error, the flag should disappear automatically.

<Check>
**Verify:** All error flags should be resolved before moving to the next step. If flags remain, the system will block submission.
</Check>

</Step>
  <Step title="Review AI improvements (sparkles)">
    Look for sparkle icons (‚ú®) indicating AI-suggested changes. Click each one to review what changed and why. Accept the change if it makes sense, or reject it if it doesn't apply to this case.

</Step>
  <Step title="Verify core claim information">
    Even without flags or sparkles, double-check these critical fields:

- Patient demographics match your practice management system
- Date of service is correct
- Provider and facility information is accurate
- Diagnosis codes support the procedures billed
- Procedure codes match the services actually provided
- Units and charges are correct

<Tip>
Keep your practice management system open in another tab for easy verification.
</Tip>

</Step>
  <Step title="Preview before submission">
    Click the **Preview** button to see exactly what will be sent to the payer. Review for any errors or missing information.

</Step>
  <Step title="Check for blocking rules">
    Before you can submit, the system runs final validation checks:

- **CHC verification:** Ensures Community Health Center compliance if applicable
- **Internal blocking rules:** Catches issues your organization has configured to prevent (e.g., claims missing certain documentation)

If a blocking rule triggers, you'll see a clear error message explaining what needs to be fixed. The **Submit** button will be disabled until you resolve the issue.

<Warning>
Blocking rules exist to protect you from submitting claims that will definitely be rejected. Don't try to work around them‚Äîfix the underlying issue instead.
</Warning>

</Step>
</Steps>

### What happens after submission

Once submitted, the claim moves into a monitoring phase:

1. **Payer acknowledgment** (1-3 business days): The payer confirms they received the claim
2. **Processing** (14-30 days typically): The payer reviews and processes the claim
3. **Payment or denial** (30-45 days typically): You receive either payment or a denial with reason

You'll monitor these claims using your **Submitted** claims view and respond to any denials or requests for additional information.

## Working with Denials

Denials happen‚Äîeven with careful review and AI validation. Your goal is to identify why claims were denied, fix the issues, and resubmit quickly to minimize revenue delays.

### Finding and filtering denials

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your Denials view">
    Switch to your saved "Recent Denials" view, or create a new filter:
    - **Encounter Status:** Denied
    - **Denial Date:** Within last 7-30 days (depending on your workflow)

This shows all denied claims that need your attention.

</Step>
  <Step title="Sort by priority">
    Sort your denials to focus on the most important ones first:

- **By claim amount (highest first):** Tackle high-dollar denials that impact revenue most
- **By denial date (oldest first):** Prevent claims from hitting timely filing limits
- **By payer:** Group denials by insurance company to spot patterns in payer-specific requirements

</Step>
  <Step title="Review denial reasons">
    The **Denial Reason** column shows why each claim was rejected. Common denial reasons include:

- **Missing or invalid information:** Incorrect patient ID, provider NPI, or authorization number
- **Coding errors:** Invalid diagnosis or procedure code combinations
- **Medical necessity:** Payer determined the service wasn't medically necessary
- **Duplicate claim:** Payer believes they already paid this claim
- **Timely filing:** Claim was submitted past the payer's deadline
- **Authorization required:** Service needed pre-authorization that wasn't obtained

<Tip>
If you see the same denial reason across multiple claims from the same payer, this indicates a systemic issue‚Äînot just individual claim problems. Flag these patterns to your team lead.
</Tip>

</Step>
</Steps>

### Analyzing individual denials

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the denied claim">
    Click the claim to view full encounter details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the denial details">
    Go to the **Payment Overview** tab to see the denial code, reason, and denied amount. Read the explanation carefully‚Äîsometimes the stated reason isn't the actual problem.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the Remittances tab">
    View the full ERA (Electronic Remittance Advice) for additional details and instructions for correcting and resubmitting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the AI copilot if needed">
    Ask the AI copilot questions like "Why was this claim denied?" or "What's the correct way to code this for [payer]?" for guidance on corrective actions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Correcting and resubmitting denials

Once you understand why the claim was denied, fix the issue and resubmit:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Make necessary corrections">
    Based on the denial reason, update the claim:
    - **Missing information:** Add missing demographics, insurance details, or authorization numbers
    - **Coding errors:** Correct invalid codes or add required modifiers
    - **Duplicate claim:** Verify if already paid, or add note explaining it's not a duplicate
    - **Timely filing:** Document original submission date if submitted on time
    - **Authorization:** Obtain required authorization if possible, or write off/bill patient
  </Step>
  <Step title="Document and resubmit">
    Add a comment in the Activity Feed explaining what was denied, what you corrected, and why. Then follow the same submission process: verify corrections, preview, and submit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
**Timely filing deadlines are strict.** Most payers give you 60-90 days from date of service. If a claim is denied and you're approaching the deadline, prioritize correcting and resubmitting immediately.
</Warning>

## Understanding AI Features

AI powers many features that catch errors and improve accuracy. This section explains how to interpret AI recommendations.

### What the sparkles (‚ú®) mean

Sparkles indicate that AI has automatically improved your claim. Common improvements include:

- **Code corrections:** Fixing invalid or outdated diagnosis/procedure codes
- **Demographic corrections:** Correcting zip codes, addresses, phone numbers
- **Modifier additions:** Adding appropriate modifiers (25, 59, GT/95, etc.) to maximize reimbursement
- **Place of service updates:** Correcting POS codes based on encounter type
- **Charge optimization:** Suggesting higher-level codes when documentation supports it

Click any sparkle to see what changed and why. Review the AI's reasoning and accept or reject based on your professional judgment.

### Evaluating AI suggestions

<Warning>
**Never accept an AI suggestion that inflates the claim beyond what's documented.** You're responsible for accuracy. Always verify against source documentation.
</Warning>

**Accept if:** AI's reasoning makes sense, documentation supports the change, and it complies with payer requirements.

**Reject if:** Documentation doesn't support it, you know payer-specific rules contradict it, or you're unsure (consult a senior biller).

---

### How blocking rules protect you

Blocking rules are AI-powered validations that prevent you from submitting claims with known problems. Unlike sparkles (which suggest improvements), blocking rules actively stop submissions until issues are fixed.

### What triggers blocking rules

<Card title="Missing required information" icon="circle-exclamation">
**Triggers when:** Critical fields are empty or invalid

**Common examples:**

- Missing patient date of birth
- Invalid or expired insurance member ID
- Missing or invalid provider NPI
- Required authorization number is blank

**Why it's blocked:** Claims with missing required info are auto-denied by payers. Submitting them wastes time and delays payment.

**How to fix:** Fill in the missing information from your practice management system, patient records, or by contacting the patient/payer directly.
</Card>

<Card title="CHC compliance violations" icon="shield-halved">
**Triggers when:** Community Health Center (CHC) compliance rules aren't met

**Common examples:**

- Missing sliding fee discount documentation for qualifying patients
- Incorrect encounter types for CHC reporting
- Services billed that aren't covered under CHC grant funding

**Why it's blocked:** CHC compliance is required for federal grant funding. Non-compliant claims put your organization at risk.

**How to fix:** Review your CHC compliance guidelines and update the claim to meet requirements. Often requires documenting patient income or changing how the service is billed.

<Info>
**Note:** CHC blocking rules only apply if your practice is a federally qualified health center (FQHC) or community health center. Non-CHC practices won't see these blocks.
</Info>
</Card>
<Card title="Known payer rejections" icon="hand">
**Triggers when:** AI detects claim characteristics that this specific payer consistently rejects

**Common examples:**

- This payer requires modifier 25 on all E/M + procedure combinations (but you didn't add it)
- This payer doesn't cover this procedure for this diagnosis code
- This payer requires pre-authorization for this service but none is documented

**Why it's blocked:** Based on historical data, this claim has an 80%+ chance of denial. Better to fix it now than resubmit later.

**How to fix:** Follow the blocking rule's guidance to add required modifiers, change coding, or obtain necessary authorizations.
</Card>

<Card title="Internal organization rules" icon="building">
**Triggers when:** Your organization has configured custom rules based on your specific policies

**Common examples:**

- High-dollar claims ($5,000+) require supervisor review before submission
- Certain procedure codes require specific documentation attached
- Services from certain providers need additional quality checks

**Why it's blocked:** Your organization has decided these claims need extra scrutiny before submission, usually to protect revenue or ensure compliance.

**How to fix:** Follow your organization's internal process (e.g., get supervisor approval, attach required documentation, complete quality checklist).
</Card>

### What to do when a claim is blocked

<Steps>
  <Step title="Read the blocking message carefully">
    When you try to submit a blocked claim, you'll see a clear error message explaining:
    - Which rule blocked the claim
    - Specifically what's wrong
    - What you need to do to fix it

Example: "Claim blocked: Missing required authorization number for procedure 99205. This payer requires authorization for new patient visits. Obtain authorization before resubmitting."

</Step>
  <Step title="Determine if you can fix it immediately">
    Ask yourself:
    - Can I find the missing information quickly (in PM system, patient chart, etc.)?
    - Is this something I have authority to fix, or do I need supervisor help?
    - Do I need information from an external source (payer, provider, patient)?

- **If you can fix it now:** Proceed to fix the issue
- **If you can't fix it now:** Assign the claim to the appropriate person (supervisor, prior auth specialist, etc.) and move to your next claim

</Step>
  <Step title="Fix the blocking issue">
    Make the required changes based on the blocking message. This might involve:
    - Adding missing data fields
    - Correcting invalid codes
    - Attaching required documentation
    - Getting supervisor approval
    - Obtaining payer authorization
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attempt submission again">
    After fixing the issue, click **Submit** again. If you fixed it correctly:
    - The blocking rule should no longer trigger
    - The claim will submit successfully

If the claim is still blocked:

- Read the new error message (it may be a different issue)
- Or verify you actually saved your corrections

<Check>
**Verify:** Submission success message should appear, and the claim status should change to **Submitted**.
</Check>

</Step>
</Steps>
<Warning>
**Never try to "work around" blocking rules** by changing codes or information in ways that don't reflect reality just to get the claim to submit. Blocking rules exist to protect you and your practice from known problems. If a rule is consistently blocking valid claims, report it to your team lead so the rule can be adjusted‚Äîdon't circumvent it.
</Warning>

### Difference between blocking rules and error flags

It's important to understand the distinction:

| Feature | Blocking Rules | Error Flags |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Purpose** | Prevent submission of claims with critical issues | Highlight potential problems for review |
| **Severity** | Critical - must be fixed | Warning - should be fixed |
| **Submission** | Cannot submit until resolved | Can sometimes submit despite flags (not recommended) |
| **Trigger** | AI detects known submission failure conditions | AI detects data quality issues |
| **Icon** | üõë Red "blocked" badge | ‚ö†Ô∏è Yellow or red warning icon |
| **Action** | Must fix before proceeding | Should fix, but system allows submission |

**Best practice:** Treat all error flags as if they were blocking rules. If AI flagged it, there's a reason. Fix it before submitting even if the system technically allows submission.

## Q&A

Common questions billers have about working with the Claims Page.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I know which claims to work on first?" icon="list-ol">
    **Prioritize in this order:**

1. **High-value claims nearing timely filing deadlines** - Sort by claim amount and age to identify these. Missing timely filing means lost revenue.
