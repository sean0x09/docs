Self-service Credentialing


<h4>Overview</h4><p>The Self-Serve Credentialing Matrix is a centralized tool designed to simplify and standardize how providers and insurance companies manage credentialing requirements. It provides an at-a-glance reference for the credentialing status of each provider‚Äìpayer relationship, ensuring clarity, compliance, and faster decision-making.</p><p>Credentialing is a critical process that validates whether providers meet the requirements of insurance companies to deliver care under their plans. Traditionally, this information is fragmented, requiring manual lookups and repeated back-and-forth. The self-serve credentialing matrix eliminates that friction by giving all stakeholders immediate visibility into credentialing statuses.</p><h4>How would it help?</h4><ul><li data-preset-tag="p"><p><strong>Efficiency</strong> ‚Äì Cuts down on time spent searching for or verifying credentialing requirements.</p></li><li data-preset-tag="p"><p><strong>Transparency</strong> ‚Äì Provides providers and payers with a shared source of truth.</p></li><li data-preset-tag="p"><p><strong>Scalability</strong> ‚Äì Supports onboarding, cross-coverage, and expanding provider networks with minimal administrative overhead.</p></li></ul><h4>Key concepts</h4><figure><table><tbody><tr><th><p><strong>Term</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Meaning</strong></p></th></tr><tr><td><p>Provider</p></td><td><p>A healthcare professional or organization (e.g., physician, nurse practitioner, clinic) delivering care to patients.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Insurance company</p></td><td><p>A payer organization that contracts with providers to cover healthcare services for its members.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Provider credentials</p></td><td><p>The qualifications, licenses, certifications, and verifications required for a provider to be approved by an insurance company.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Credentialing group</p></td><td><p>A set of providers managed together for credentialing purposes, often by specialty, practice, or organizational affiliation.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Credentialed</p></td><td><p>Indicates whether a provider is fully approved and recognized by the insurance company to deliver covered services.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Cosigner required</p></td><td><p>Shows whether the provider can deliver services only under supervision, requiring a credentialed provider‚Äôs cosignature (e.g., trainees, newly licensed practitioners).</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Credentialing type</p></td><td><p>Type of credentialing.
