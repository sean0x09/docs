Turn off Patient Texts

<h4>At a Glance</h4><p>By default all patients receive their bills via text message and email. However for various reasons, some patients prefer not to be contacted this way. In these cases, here’s how to turn off text messages for an individual patient.</p><h4>Here’s How to Do It</h4><p><a href="https://athelas.helpkit.so/how-to-guides/mx4v4WBLUG3MERujomrLhL/how-to-find-and-edit-a-patient%E2%80%99s-profile/k7Tz4h9MSHwdJ8LzVkJJCS">Navigate to the patient’s profile</a>.</p><p>You’ll see whether automatic texts are enabled or disabled below the patient’s basic information. Click <code>Edit</code> to change that status.</p><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/tIr2YHojAkRpFd4Zqnyl9XywVDE.png"><h4>And You’re All Set!</h4><p>This patient will no longer receive text message communications unless you manually trigger them, or you change this setting in the future.</p><h4>Frequently Asked Questions</h4><blockquote><p><strong>Does turning text messages off prevent email statements from being sent as well?</strong></p></blockquote><p>No, it does not prevent emails. If you have an email address on file for a patient, and your practice has emails activate in you <a href="https://insights.athelas.com/v2/pr-statements">patient statements settings</a>, then patients will continue to get statements by email even with text messaging turned off.</p>
