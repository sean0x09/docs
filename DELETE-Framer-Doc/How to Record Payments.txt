How to Record Payments

<h3>At a Glance</h3><p>Sometimes you need a record of an external payment to balance an encounter without charging a patient again. This is particularly useful if your organization has recently transitioned to Athelas from another billing company.</p><p><br></p><h3>Here’s How to Do It</h3><p><strong>The short version:</strong></p><ol><li data-preset-tag="p"><p><strong>Create available credits</strong> in the exact amount that was previously paid.</p></li><li data-preset-tag="p"><p><strong>Create a miscellaneous charge</strong> in that amount.</p></li><li data-preset-tag="p"><p><strong>Apply the credits you just created to that charge</strong> to balance it out.</p></li></ol><h3>Full Walkthrough</h3><p>Navigate to the patient’s profile.</p><p>Switch to the ‘Credits’ tab, then select <code>Edit Credits</code>.</p><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/GJiqyAJaUYoPFaZ7dZYQFcZOQw.png"><p>Fill in the exact amount previously paid, then add a note explaining why you’re creating these credits. Be sure to check the box for <code>Available Credits</code> so that you can use them now, and they’re not tied to a date of service.</p><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/mi666mUF2q4bxmF4NHg6EW7AGwo.png"><p>The credits will appear in the patient’s ‘Credits’ tab. You can click the arrow next to them to see the details you filled in.</p><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/Q4JDpNaVgxDB1HMUoCCtU6UpI8.png"><p>Now, switch to the ‘Charges’ tab and click <code>+Miscellaneous Charge</code>.</p><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/5ScwEmEKj6ydNcNf4c5gxUsfGRM.png"><p>A modal will appear with fields for you to input information.</p><p>You’ll notice the warning at the top of this modal indicating how it can and cannot be used. Fortunately, this situation requires that both a payment and corresponding charge are created, so we’re good to go!</p><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/4hXgN6xQvcBwwHgkHQrplCBaPaw.png"><p>Fill in the patient’s facility and current provider.</p><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/iW9Rdf2eteFw6OMxwoEqwaFCU.png"><p>For the ‘Line Item’ section, you may need to create a line item for recording external payments. </p><h5>Here's a walkthrough on how to do that. Feel free to skip ahead if you've already set this up.</h5><ol><li data-preset-tag="p"><p>Navigate to <a href="https://insights.athelas.com/v2/patient-responsibility-settings">PR Settings</a>. You’ll land on the ‘General’ tab by default.</p></li><li data-preset-tag="p"><p>Scroll down to the ‘Custom Payment Types’ section.</p><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/9yQSI2ScSUEAR4espXIqyglm4.png"><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/zxuCdhRR9xaiRwVw4vUjG68SyE.png"></li><li data-preset-tag="p"><p>Click the <code>+</code> to create a custom payment line.</p></li><li data-preset-tag="p"><p>Enter a name like ‘Record of payment taken externally,’ set the Type to ‘Standard,’ and leave the default charge amount at 0. Click <code>Create</code>.</p><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/b9KipdGAajmx7FYzLJaVaWTAzeM.png"></li><li data-preset-tag="p"><p>You can see your new line item in the list in PR Settings.</p><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/d4pSpZA9c1jEWcdH5En0w208.png"></li></ol><p>Done! Now you can select this option in the ‘Line Item’ menu of the <code>Miscellaneous Charge</code> modal.</p><p><br></p><h5>If you already have the line item described above, great!</h5><p>Select it and enter the exact amount of credits you created in the ‘Pay Amount’ field.</p><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/iW9Rdf2eteFw6OMxwoEqwaFCU.png"><p>Next, check the box to <code>Apply Credits</code> and the option to <code>Pay with Patient Credits</code> will select automatically.</p><p>You can add a note to this payment if you like.</p><p>Once everything looks correct, click <code>Confirm</code>.</p><img alt="" src="https://framerusercontent.com/images/KOrP8quNe8oMFB9XNo5QilwsWto.png"><p>Confirm the transaction once more, and then you’ll have the opportunity to download, print, or send a receipt to the patient via text or email. We advise against sending a receipt in this case, as that would cause the patient some confusion and it might look like they were just charged twice.</p>
